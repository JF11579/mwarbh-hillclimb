About the Race

The Course

  • 7.6 miles, entirely uphill
  • Start: Auto Road Toll House (base, ~1,600 ft)
  • Finish: Mt. Washington Summit (6,288 ft)
  • 4,688 ft of climbing
  • Average grade: ~12%
  • Maximum grade: ~22%
  • Unpaved sections in upper miles

Race Day — August 15, 2026

  • Rain Date: Sunday, August 16, 2026
  • Check-in: Friday Aug 14, 3–8 PM
  • Check-in: Saturday Aug 15, 5–6:30 AM
  • Staging opens: 5:30 AM
  • Mass start: 7:00 AM
  • Course closes: ~12:00 PM
  • Awards ceremony: ~1:00 PM at base

Registration Timeline

  • Opens: Spring 2026 (via BikeReg)
  • Early Bird: Limited spots, first-come
  • General: Until sellout or July 31
  • Waitlist: Available after sellout
  • Cap: ~600 riders

Categories

  • Open Men / Open Women
  • Age groups: 19–29, 30–39, 40–49, 50–59, 60+
  • Tandem division
  • Singlespeed / Fixed gear
  • Clydesdale (200+ lbs)

What to Expect

  • No descent — all riders transported down
  • Support vehicles allowed on course
  • Feed zones at miles 4 and 6
  • Medical support on course
  • Summit finish — world-famous views

Registration

Registration is managed through BikeReg.com. Spots fill fast — typically within hours of opening.

Register on BikeReg.com ↗

Eligibility

  • Minimum age: 18 on race day
  • Valid USAC license or one-day license (available at check-in)
  • Road, mountain, gravel, cyclocross, or fixed-gear bikes permitted
  • No e-bikes
  • Helmet required (CPSC or CE EN 1078 certified)

Fees (approximate)

  • Early bird: ~$70
  • General: ~$80–$90
  • One-day USAC license: ~$15 (if needed)
  • No day-of registration

What's Included

  • Race number and timing chip
  • Post-race meal at summit
  • Transport down from summit
  • Finisher certificate
  • Category prizes / awards

Transfers & Refunds

  • Transfers accepted until ~2 weeks before race
  • No refunds after registration closes
  • Waitlist transfers honored as slots open
  • Contact: info@mwarbh.org

Gear Check

  • Bikes checked by officials at check-in
  • Working brakes required
  • Lights recommended for low-visibility summit
  • Warm layers — summit temp often 20°F cooler than base

Support Crew

  • Support vehicles must register separately
  • One vehicle per rider maximum
  • Pass required for course access
  • Parking limited — carpool encouraged
